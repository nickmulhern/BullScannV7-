What BullScann Does

BullScann scans a fixed list of 18 stocks and ETFs every session, scores each one on trend, momentum, and key price levels, and ranks them so you can see the best setups first. When a setup looks strong, the Trade Desk runs an automated three-step review and hands you a finished trade plan — entry, strike, targets, and stop — ready to act on.

The Daily Routine — 3 Steps

1

Run the Firewall. Go to the Firewall tab, tap Open Claude, run the prompt, and paste the reply back. This checks the overall market mood and flags any stocks with risky news today. Do this once per session before you scan.

2

Run the Scan. Go to the Scan tab and tap Run Scan. BullScann pulls live prices and grades every stock in the list A through C, long or short, based on trend and momentum.

3

Send a setup to the Trade Desk. Open any graded card and tap the Trade Desk button. Three AI reviewers — a Trader, a Risk Desk, and a Trade Master — go back and forth on the idea and hand you a final ticket: direction, entry, strike, targets, and stop.

Reading a Scan Card

The letter grade (A, B, C) is a quick read on setup quality — A is the strongest. The number below it (out of 100) is the underlying score. Long means the system sees upside; short means it sees downside. Tap any card to expand it and see price levels, support and resistance, and risk flags.

Daily tab = swing setups (weeks-long). 5-min tab = short-term setups (days). The Trade Desk button only appears for the timeframe you're currently viewing.

Reading a Trade Desk Ticket

GO means the setup passed review and is ready to consider. NO-GO means the Risk Desk found a problem serious enough to kill it. CONDITIONAL means it's worth a look but with caveats — read the rationale before acting.

Trigger Price is where the trade gets confirmed. T1 and T2 are profit targets. Stop is where you'd exit if it goes wrong. Tap "Show full analysis" if you want to see the full back-and-forth between all three reviewers.

Why the Firewall Has to Be Fresh

The Trade Desk won't run unless the Firewall was saved today and within the last 8 hours. This stops yesterday's market read from quietly feeding into today's trades. If you see a stale warning, just re-run the Firewall — takes under a minute.

Good to Know

  • Nothing in this system is financial advice — it's a research and screening tool. You make the final call on every trade.
  • The options chain isn't connected yet, so grades are based on price/technical data only, not options liquidity or implied volatility.
  • If a scan or Trade Desk call fails, it's usually a connection issue — try again before assuming something's broken.
